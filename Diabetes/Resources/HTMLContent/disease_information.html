Living with a rare adipose disorder is a challenge because of the many things patients are asked to do: take new medications, wear compression garments, use lypmphatic pumps, lose weight, increase their activity level, and eat a healthy diet. If left unchecked, rare adipose disorders can have serious long-term complications, including:

  • Pain
  • Autonomic Nervous System Disfunction
  • Emotional Disturbances
  • Rapid Weight Gain
  • Disability
  • The need to have lipomas or adipose removed due to poor blood circulation, immobility, pain or nerve pressure
